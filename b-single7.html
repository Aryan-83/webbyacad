Ultimate Guide to Dominate Google SERPs

Increase the authority of your domain

Domain authority is a statistic that Google used to rate a website’s reputation a few years ago. Google stopped publishing this number and its page-specific counterpart, page authority, a while ago, but it’s still certainly part of their algorithm in some form or another.

Moz devised its own domain authority algorithm to fill the void left by Google’s official number. This method attempts to reverse engineer the Google algorithm in order to evaluate how trustworthy a specific website is in Google’s eyes.

Moz’s domain authority algorithm, like Google’s, rates websites from 1 to 100 depending on a variety of characteristics such as backlinks, root domains, and the site’s history.

Increase your Google ranking

As you might expect, increasing your domain authority requires a lot of time and effort. Google, and by extension Moz, want to see consistent, relevant content that fits the needs of searchers. Most of the biggest magazines have been around for years and have hundreds, if not thousands, of pages, articles, and visitors on their websites. The good news is that increasing your domain authority is easier the lower your domain authority is. It’s similar to rising up in a video game—the first few levels are the easiest, and it gets increasingly difficult as you progress. Almost any website with at least some high-quality content can achieve a domain authority of 10 or above. Getting over that point, on the other hand, can be difficult.

Obtain Additional Backlinks

Backlinks are an important element of how Google judges the worth of your content, as previously stated. The concept is simple: if a website or webpage contains useful information, other websites will naturally want to link to it. The main issue is that most people don’t look very hard for the good stuff. They frequently skim through the first few search results on a page, find a credible source, and link to it.

As a result, high-ranking material attracts more backlinks. Content that does not rank well tends to fade into obscurity over time. Content marketing is one of the most effective techniques to get backlinks to your website. The more high-value material you provide, the more likely it will be discovered and linked.

Everything should be optimized for the right keywords.

Your keyword approach is crucial to achieving high rankings on Google’s search engine results page (SERP). If you’re ranking for the wrong keywords, it doesn’t matter how good you rank. If you want to appear in the SERPs right away but only have a domain authority of 16, you’ll want to find the lowest-competition keywords you can. Take a look at the low-competition keywords and see which ones have the best search potential out of the bunch, and start there.
